Event classification framework

Phase 1

Preconditions

Structural vulnerabilities that precede visible authoritarian emergence. Measurable, slow-moving.

Economic preconditions Inequality, unemployment shocks, deindustrialization, perceived relative deprivation
Social preconditions Polarization metrics, erosion of cross-party institutional trust, identity threat narratives
Institutional preconditions Norm erosion, gatekeeping failures, legislative abdication to executive
Phase 2

Emergence

The active phase where authoritarian patterns become visible through specific mechanisms.

Leadership methods Charismatic appeal, manufactured crisis, scapegoating, emotional/nationalist rhetoric
Coercive mechanisms Paramilitary or law-enforcement weaponization, detention without due process, surveillance infrastructure
Narrative control Propaganda patterns, media delegitimization, information environment manipulation
Exploitation of existing systems Legal mechanisms used against their intent, procedural manipulation
Phase 3

Consolidation

Structural changes that entrench power and make reversal harder.

Judiciary restructuring Court packing, removal of independent judges, constitutional reinterpretation
Legislative capture Supermajority consolidation, opposition suppression, electoral rule changes
Border and population control Movement restrictions, citizenship policy, minority targeting
Foreign policy as domestic tool Nationalist framing of international events for domestic legitimacy
Framework informed by Levitsky & Ziblatt, V-Dem, Freedom House, Polity5.  ·  Every indicator is defined and sourced.  ·  Analysis, not advocacy.
