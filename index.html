Executive order directs DOJ to investigate judges who rule against administration

A presidential executive order instructed the Department of Justice to open conduct reviews of federal judges who have issued rulings blocking administration immigration and deportation policies. Legal scholars noted the order uses existing DOJ procedural authority but applies it in a manner that targets judicial independence — a documented pattern in comparative authoritarian cases.

Historical parallel — Hungary (Orbán, 2011–2013): After gaining a supermajority, the Fidesz government lowered the mandatory retirement age for judges, forcing out roughly 274 senior judges and replacing them with loyalists. Formal legal mechanisms were used; no overt rule-breaking was required. The EU later ruled the measure violated EU law, but the judiciary had already been reshaped.
Source: Reuters, Apr 9 2025  ·  DOJ press release
